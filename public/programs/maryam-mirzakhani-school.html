About the program

The Maryam Mirzakhani School of Mathematics and Science is a 3-day foundational program for primary students (Grade 5 and below). Inspired by Maryam Mirzakhani's playful, visual, and narrative approach to STEM, the curriculum builds deep conceptual understanding and a lasting passion for mathematics and science in early-age learners.

Designed for students preparing for the Bangladesh Mathematics and Science Olympiad (BdMSO) 2026, the syllabus prioritises deep conceptual intuition, spatial reasoning, and creative problem-solving over rote memorisation - delivered across 6 mathematics and 6 science sessions, with 10.5 hours of teaching in each subject.

How we teach

Principle 01

Every student receives a sketchbook. Complex equations and scientific systems are drawn out first, focusing on visual structures rather than memorising symbols.

Principle 02

We prioritise why a system works - modular arithmetic clocks, for example - over speedy mental-arithmetic drills.

Principle 03

Science concepts like genetic traits or the carbon cycle are framed as natural mysteries, positioning the student as an active detective.

Principle 04

Material focuses on the structural principles tested in BdMSO - pigeonhole principle, combinations, Delta geography, basic genetics, force vectors - presented in an intuitive, age-appropriate way.

Curriculum & schedule

Cohort 1 runs over three days at MASLab, Dhanmondi. Mathematics covers 6 sessions across the themes below; science moves through 6 topic-led sessions. Each subject totals 10.5 hours of teaching.
