Syllabus

A short summary of the math and science topics covered at the National Round and TST.

⬇ Syllabus.pdf 1.8 MB

📐 Mathematics

  • Number theory, place value, factors & multiples
  • Fractions, decimals, ratios and percentages
  • Geometry — shapes, symmetry, area & perimeter
  • Algebraic reasoning & word problems
  • Combinatorics & basic counting
  • Data interpretation & simple statistics

🔬 Science

  • Matter, states and changes
  • Forces, motion and simple machines
  • Electricity, light and sound
  • Life sciences — cells, plants, human body
  • Earth & space — weather, ecosystems, planets
  • Experimental method and data

Regulations

Official eligibility rules, exam conduct, and BdMSO code of honour.

⬇ Regulations.pdf 740 KB
  • Open to students of Class 3 to 6 studying in Bangladesh (national or equivalent curriculum).
  • Each student may sit for one subject stream in a given cycle — Mathematics or Science.
  • Calculators, phones and external aids are not permitted in the National Round or TST.
  • The Top 15 TST finalists receive portal notifications; declining students are replaced from the next eligible.
  • The final IMSO delegation is confirmed after logistics and airfare are settled.

Guide for Parents

What you need to know before your child begins.

What is BdMSO?

The official qualifying olympiad to select primary-school students to represent Bangladesh at IMSO — the International Math & Science Olympiad.

Time commitment

Plan for 2–4 hours per week during prep, plus the residential camps. Camps span 5–14 days and are fully supervised.

Selection process

Register → Qualifying Round → Residential Camp → TST → Preparatory Camp → International delegation. See the Road to IMSO.

06

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible to participate in BdMSO?

Students of Class 3 to 6 enrolled in any school in Bangladesh. Both national and international curriculums are welcome.

What is the registration fee?

A nominal registration fee applies for the National Round. Fee waivers are available — please contact us via the help desk.

Is my child too young to start?

The Foundation Program and Lab Day Workshops are open to Class 3 and above. Start early with these if your child is new to olympiad-style thinking.

How are final delegates selected?

Through a transparent multi-stage process — Qualifying Round, Residential Camp performance, and Team Selection Test. Numerical scores are not published publicly.

What language are exams conducted in?

Bilingual (Bangla and English). Students may answer in either.
