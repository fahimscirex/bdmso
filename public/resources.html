Regulations

Official eligibility rules, exam conduct, and BdMSO code of conduct.

⬇ Regulations.pdf 740 KB
  • Open to primary school students under 13 years old, enrolled in Grade 6 or lower as of September 30, 2026, with a birth date on or after September 30, 2013.
  • Contestants may register for Mathematics, Science, or both — no categories based on class or grade level.
  • There are no regional rounds — BdMSO features a direct National Round.
  • Awards are granted in only one subject per participant based on scores, even if registered for both.
  • Approximately two thirds of all participants receive medals in a gold:silver:bronze ratio of 1:2:3 per subject.
  • All questions and answers at IMSO must be in English.

Frequently Asked Questions

⬇ FAQ.docx
Who is eligible to participate in BdMSO?

BdMSO is open to students across Bangladesh who are under 13 years old, born on or after September 30, 2013, and enrolled in Grade 6 or lower as of September 30, 2026.

What subjects can I participate in?

Contestants can participate in Mathematics, Science, or both. There are no categories based on school class or grade level.

How do I register and what is the fee?

Registration is online at festive.rocks/sh/bdmso. The fee is 1,000 BDT for one subject, or 1,500 BDT for both Mathematics and Science. Payment is made by purchasing tickets at the registration link.

When and where is the National Round?

The BdMSO National Round is held as a direct national competition — there are no regional rounds. The event day includes an inauguration ceremony, one-hour exams per subject, a Science Experiment Session, Q&A, and a Prize Giving ceremony.

What prizes are awarded?

Approximately two thirds of participants receive gold, silver, and bronze medals in a 1:2:3 ratio per subject. All medalists receive a winner's t-shirt and Certificate of Achievement. A Champion Trophy is awarded to the top scorer in each subject. Every attendee receives a Certificate of Participation.

Can a participant win awards in both subjects?

No. While contestants may register for both subjects, awards are granted in only one subject per participant based on their scores.

How are IMSO team members selected?

Top scorers from the National Round are invited to a non-residential National Camp. From there, top performers proceed to a National Team Selection Test. The top six from each subject form the national team representing Bangladesh at IMSO.

What is IMSO and how does BdMSO connect to it?

IMSO (International Mathematics and Science Olympiad) is a prestigious annual competition for primary school students under 13 from around the world. BdMSO is Bangladesh's official national qualifying round for IMSO. The national team selected through BdMSO will represent Bangladesh at the 23rd IMSO in 2026.

What are the costs for IMSO participation?

Selected national delegates pay a registration fee covering travel, visa, accommodation, and local transport. One parent may accompany their child for an additional fee of approximately $650 USD payable directly to the IMSO organizers.

How should my child prepare?

Follow the official BdMSO syllabus. It is recommended to practice elementary-level creative problems and study IMSO sample questions and past papers. Elementary-level Mathematics and Science textbooks are also useful.
