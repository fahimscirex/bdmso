Regulations

Official eligibility rules, exam conduct, and BdMSO code of conduct.

⬇ Download Detailed Regulations 522 KB
  • Open to primary school students aged 13 or younger, enrolled in Grade 6 or lower as of October 1, 2026, born on or after October 1, 2013.
  • The Olympiad is a single two-hour combined exam covering both Mathematics and Science sections - there are no separate math/science registrations.
  • BdMSO operates as a single category for IMSO qualification. A separate Quiz Competition may be held for Grade 3 or lower as encouragement.
  • Approximately the top 20–30% of registered participants are designated Winners and receive a medal and Certificate of Achievement.
  • Award classes: All-Round Winners (top 5% by combined score), Mathematics Winners, and Science Winners.
  • English is the official language for all examination papers, instructions, and communications.
  • Olympiad registration fees range from 1,000–1,500 BDT. National Camp fees range from 3,000–5,000 BDT. TST fee is 1,000 BDT per subject. International participation fee is announced after the TST.

Frequently Asked Questions

⬇ FAQ.docx
Who is eligible to participate in BdMSO?

BdMSO is open to students across Bangladesh who are 13 years old or younger, born on or after October 1, 2013, and enrolled in Grade 6 or lower as of October 1, 2026. Students in the British curriculum (or similar streams) who are promoted to Grade 7 in July/August 2026 are not eligible.

What is the format of the Olympiad?

The BdMSO National Round is a single 120-minute combined exam covering both Mathematics and Science (50 + 50 = 100 marks). It is held at multiple regional venues, simultaneously or on different dates. The day includes the National Anthem, the exam, a lunch break, a cultural program, and a prize-giving ceremony.

How do I register and what is the fee?

Registration is online at www.bdmso.org. The Olympiad registration fee is 1000 BDT per student. Subsequent stages have separate fees: National Residential Camp 3,000–5,000 BDT, TST 1,000 BDT per subject, and the international participation fee is announced after the TST.

What prizes are awarded?

Approximately the top 20–30% of participants are designated Winners and receive a medal and Certificate of Achievement. Winners are classified as All-Round Winners (top 5% combined), Mathematics Winners, or Science Winners. A Champion Trophy is awarded to the highest scorer in each section, and every attendee receives a Certificate of Participation.

Is financial aid available?

Yes. The organizing committee may consider partial waivers of up to 50% on Olympiad, Camp, or TST fees for gifted students with significant financial need. Independent sponsorship is also encouraged.

How are IMSO team members selected?

Olympiad winners (approximately the top 20%) are invited to a three-day National Residential Camp. About a week after the camp, the Team Selection Test (TST) - a day-long evaluation per subject - determines the team. The top six per subject (12 total: 6 Math + 6 Science) form the primary national team, with 2 Tutors and 1 Team Leader, completing the standard 15-member delegation.

What is IMSO and how does BdMSO connect to it?

IMSO (International Mathematics and Science Olympiad) is a prestigious annual competition for primary school students under 13 from around the world. BdMSO is Bangladesh's official national qualifying round for IMSO. The national team selected through BdMSO will represent Bangladesh at the 23rd IMSO in 2026.

What are the costs for IMSO participation?

Selected national delegates pay a registration fee covering travel, visa, accommodation, and local transport. One parent may accompany their child for an additional fee of approximately $650 USD payable directly to the IMSO organizers.

How should my child prepare?

Follow the official BdMSO syllabus. It is recommended to practice elementary-level creative problems and study IMSO sample questions and past papers. Elementary-level Mathematics and Science textbooks are also useful.
