Last Sunday, the ACI Centre in Tejgaon transformed into a vibrant hub of curiosity for primary school students across Dhaka. The ACI Medhabi Carnival brought together young learners for a day packed with interactive challenges, creative activities, and a once-in-a-lifetime face-off against a chess Grandmaster.

## Chess Against a Grandmaster

The day's headline moment was a simultaneous chess exhibition where students had the opportunity to play against a titled Grandmaster. Students who had never touched a chess board sat across the board with as much focus as seasoned players. For many, it was a lesson in thinking many moves ahead - a skill that translates directly into mathematics and science problem-solving.

## What Else Was on Offer

The carnival floor was packed with stations designed to spark curiosity:

- **Art stations** - students explored geometric patterns and symmetry through drawing and colouring
- **Tangram puzzles** - spatial reasoning challenges that had participants fully absorbed
- **Book reading corner** - a quiet zone stocked with age-appropriate science and math books, with volunteers on hand to guide reading
- **Robotics demos** - BdMSO volunteers showcased basic robotics kits and let students try building simple circuits
- **Telescopes** - a personal favourite: students queued to get a magnified look at the sky and nearby structures, sparking questions about optics and space science

## Why Events Like This Matter

BdMSO exists not just to select a national team, but to build a culture of curiosity at the primary level. Events like the ACI Medhabi Carnival show students that mathematics and science are not confined to textbooks - they are alive in games, puzzles, machines, and the sky above.

A huge thank you to ACI and everyone who made the event possible. We look forward to bringing more students into these experiences ahead of BdMSO 2026.
