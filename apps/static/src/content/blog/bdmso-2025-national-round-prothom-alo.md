*This post is a summary of [press coverage published by Prothom Alo](https://en.prothomalo.com/youth/education/5w0flop9aq) on 22 August 2025.*

Bangladesh held its inaugural Mathematics and Science Olympiad (BdMSO) for primary-level students on 22 August 2025, aiming to foster curiosity and build strong foundational knowledge in mathematics and science. The national round was hosted at **St. Joseph Higher Secondary School** in Dhaka and drew over 200 participants from across the country - more than 150 students in mathematics and more than 100 in science.

After the written exams, organizers entertained participants with robotics and drone exhibitions and a magic show.

## Distinguished Guests

The day's distinguished attendees included:

- **Brother Leo James Pereira** - Principal, St. Joseph Higher Secondary School
- **Sarwat Reza** - Cambridge University Press and Assessment, Country Lead
- **Jahangir Masud** - Principal, Legend International School
- **Professor Farzana Alam** - European University of Bangladesh
- **Jannatul Ferdous Sigma** - Pearson Bangladesh, Regional Development Manager

Brother Leo encouraged the participants: *"This experience will be invaluable on your journey to fulfilling your dreams."*

Sarwat Reza affirmed that *"competitions like this play a vital role in nurturing students' knowledge and skills."*

## Results

Fifty-two students were awarded medals across both subjects:

- **Mathematics:** 4 gold, 9 silver, 13 bronze
- **Science:** 5 gold, 9 silver, 12 bronze

### Champions

- **Mathematics:** Md Nabeeh Hossain (Grade 5, St. Joseph Higher Secondary School)
- **Science:** Syed Ayaan Isa (Grade 4, Lighthouse International School)

## Next Steps

Six mathematics and six science winners will represent Bangladesh at the **22nd International Mathematics and Science Olympiad (IMSO)**, scheduled for **4-10 October 2025 in Kedah, Malaysia**, with nearly 300 students from 23 countries participating.

## Organizers and Partners

BdMSO 2025 was organized by the **Bangladesh Open Source Network (BdOSN)** and the **Society for the Popularization of Science, Bangladesh (SPSB)**, supported by **MassLab**, **Tems Academy**, and **Reflective Teen**.
