When we think of advanced mathematics and science, we often picture rigid rules and dry memorization. But for Maryam Mirzakhani, the first woman to win the Fields Medal - the highest honor in mathematics - it was an entirely different world.

As a child, Maryam didn't dream of numbers; she wanted to be a writer. When she eventually discovered mathematics, she realized it wasn't an abstract chore - it was a beautiful form of storytelling. Maryam was famous for her incredibly playful and visual approach to problem-solving. She would spread giant sheets of white paper across the floor, drawing elaborate doodles, shapes, and diagrams to find hidden patterns. She beautifully described tackling a tough problem as *"being lost in a jungle and trying to use all the knowledge you can gather to come up with new tricks."*

This is exactly why we are hosting the **Maryam Mirzakhani School of Mathematics and Science** for primary students (Grade 5 and below).

We want to show early-age learners that STEM is a canvas for imagination. Inspired by Maryam's visual, creative mindset, this 3-day foundational intervention moves away from rote learning. Instead, it builds deep conceptual understanding, sharpens analytical thinking, and instills a lifelong passion for discovery. Give your child an early, competitive edge to unlock their full potential and prepare them for the exciting academic journey ahead.
