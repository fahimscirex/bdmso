## About this program

The Maryam Mirzakhani School of Mathematics and Science is a 3-day foundational program for primary students (Class 5 and below). Inspired by Maryam Mirzakhani's playful, visual, and narrative approach to STEM - the first woman to win the Fields Medal - the curriculum builds deep conceptual understanding and a lasting passion for mathematics and science in early-age learners.

Designed for students preparing for the Bangladesh Mathematics and Science Olympiad (BdMSO) 2026, the syllabus prioritises deep conceptual intuition, spatial reasoning, and creative problem-solving over rote memorisation - delivered across 6 mathematics and 6 science sessions, with 10.5 hours of teaching in each subject.

## How we teach

- **Draw it first** - every student gets a sketchbook; complex equations and scientific systems are drawn out, focusing on visual structures rather than memorising symbols.
- **Why over speed** - we prioritise *why* a system works (modular-arithmetic clocks, for example) over mental-arithmetic drills.
- **Student as detective** - science concepts like genetic traits or the carbon cycle are framed as natural mysteries to investigate.
- **Olympiad-aligned** - material focuses on the structural principles BdMSO tests (pigeonhole principle, combinations, delta geography, basic genetics, force vectors), presented in an age-appropriate way.

## Curriculum & schedule

Cohort 1 ran over three days at MASLab, Dhanmondi. Mathematics covers 6 sessions; science moves through 6 topic-led sessions. Each subject totals 10.5 hours of teaching.

### Day One · 23 May 2026
- **Science · 9:00-10:30 AM - Scientific Foundations:** the scientific method, units/scale/estimation, data interpretation & graphing.
- **Science · 11:00 AM-1:00 PM - The Living World:** living vs non-living things, cells & organelles, classifying organisms, photosynthesis/respiration/transpiration.
- **Mathematics · 2:00-3:30 PM - The Language of Numbers:** prime & composite numbers, prime factorization, number systems.
- **Mathematics · 4:00-6:00 PM - Chasing Patterns & Remainder Clocks:** divisibility rules, modular arithmetic, arithmetic & geometric sequences.

### Day Two · 24 May 2026
- **Mathematics · 9:00-10:30 AM - Symmetry, Mirrors & Flatland:** properties of 2D shapes, transformations, angle relationships.
- **Mathematics · 11:00 AM-1:00 PM - Architects of the 3D World:** solid figures, nets of cubes & parallelepipeds, surface area & volume.
- **Science · 2:00-3:30 PM - Our Earth & Environment:** Bengal Delta formation, climate change & natural disasters, ecology, ecosystems, food webs.
- **Science · 4:00-6:00 PM - Forces, Energy & the Cosmos:** forces & their effects, energy conversions, Earth's rotation, moon phases & eclipses, light & sound waves.

### Day Three · 25 May 2026
- **Mathematics · 9:00-10:30 AM - The Art of Counting Without Counting:** systematic counting & ordered pairs, permutations & combinations, pigeonhole principle, visual probability.
- **Mathematics · 11:00 AM-1:00 PM - The Final Maze, Logic & Recreational Math:** logic grids & deductive puzzles, cryptarithms & magic squares, working backward.
- **Science · 2:00-3:30 PM - Matter, Heat & Electromagnetism:** atoms & molecules, states of matter, heat transfer, electricity & magnetism.
- **Science · 4:00-6:00 PM - The Human Body & Biotechnology:** organ systems & skeletal structure, the nervous system, nutrition & disease prevention, genetics, biotechnology & GMOs.

Each session block includes a short snacks break; lunch is provided for both-subject participants.
